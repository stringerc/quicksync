LEGAL STANDARD
The standard of care for Georgia attorneys requires each attorney to use such skill,
prudence, and diligence as lawyers of ordinary skill and capacity commonly possess and
exercise in the performance of tasks which they undertake. Kellos v. Sawilowsky,
254 Ga. 4, 5, 325 S.E.2d 757 (1985). Georgia law requires three elements for legal
malpractice: (1) employment of the defendant attorney; (2) failure of the attorney to
exercise ordinary care, skill, and diligence; and (3) that such negligence was the
proximate cause of damage to the plaintiff. Rogers v. Norvell, 174 Ga. App. 453, 457,
330 S.E.2d 392 (1985); Mauldin v. Weinstock, 201 Ga. App. 514, 518(4), 411 S.E.2d
370 (1991). The breach inquiry is whether the attorney exercised reasonable care in the
circumstances — not whether the attorney subjectively acted in good faith. Cox-Ott v.
Barnes & Thornburg, LLP, 2025 Ga. LEXIS 98 (Ga. May 13, 2025) (Georgia Supreme
Court eliminating judgmental immunity doctrine; reframing inquiry as whether attorney
exercised reasonable care in forming and implementing professional judgment). It is a
fundamental duty of counsel to keep cases diligently prosecuted and take whatever
action is necessary to protect the client’s interests. Venable v. Block, 138 Ga. App. 215,
218–219, 225 S.E.2d 755 (1976). Proof of damages requires proof that the plaintiff
would have prevailed in the original matter but for the attorney’s negligence. White v.
Rolley, 225 Ga. App. 467, 468(1), 484 S.E.2d 83 (1997). Counter-Plaintiff’s own
successful independent discovery — obtaining the body-worn camera footage that
Counter-Defendant was explicitly instructed to obtain and failed to obtain — proves the
evidence existed, was accessible through proper discovery, and had substantial value
in the interconnected proceedings.

## Intent (one sentence)

**Elevate the pasted pleading (Answer, defenses, counterclaim, and internal reference material) to the tone, structure, and precision expected of elite Georgia appellate practice—without treating buried “verify citations” appendix language as the primary task.**

---

## Success criteria (checklist)

Mark pass/fail when evaluating a Lawbot reply to this baseline.

> **Syntax note:** These use the same **Markdown task-list** pattern as GitHub READMEs (`- [ ]` / `- [x]`) — the style you see in repos like [Claude Code](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code) and other projects. It is a **human review** rubric, not a dependency on that tool or repo.

The Lawbot web UI includes an optional **interactive** version of this checklist (sidebar) so you can tick items after you read each reply.

- [ ] **Primary deliverable** is substantive drafting help: revised wording, tighter structure, clearer headings, stronger framing—or a prioritized list of specific edits—not a vault chunk matrix as the main body.
- [ ] **Opening instruction wins** — the first lines ask for “top .01% appellate attorney” quality; the reply does not reframe the whole response as **citation verification** unless you explicitly asked for verify-first.
- [ ] **Appendix handled correctly** — “HOW TO VERIFY THESE CITATIONS” and similar text in the paste must not override classify/strengthen behavior (buried instruction ≠ user intent).
- [ ] **No pointless repetition** — if citations are discussed, **dedupe** (unique authorities + status), not dozens of identical rows.
- [ ] **Consistency** — no contradictory “matches / not verified” for the same cite without explaining the distinction (e.g. different chunks, different sub-issues).
- [ ] **Proportion** — optional short citation-risk or vault note is fine; it must not dwarf the rewrite/strengthen content.

## Must not (automatic fail if triggered)

- Main reply is a **long numbered list** of “[statute] … Matches / Not verified” keyed to SOURCE CHUNKS when the user’s **opening** is rewrite/strengthen.
- Reply states **“This is a citation verification task”** (or equivalent) as the scope for this baseline message.
- **Hundreds of near-duplicate** citation rows with no deduplication.
- **Internal inconsistency** — same statute toggling match/not verified across rows with no rationale.
