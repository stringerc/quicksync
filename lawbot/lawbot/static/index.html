Pre-filing checklist (high ROI)
  1. SOC line-by-line: After you obtain the Statement of Claim, answer paragraph-by-paragraph — fee cases often turn on notice and admissions. Use the button below to insert a prompt template, then paste the SOC.
  2. Expert / O.C.G.A. § 9-11-9.1: Decide with counsel timing and expert scope (standard of care for discovery in your case type); treat it as a procedural to-do, not a DIY legal conclusion.
  3. Verified analogues: Shepardize paid research; paste short excerpts into Add to vault so the next reply can quote real text in the Editor’s overview.
  4. Lawbot settings for drafting: Keep case search on, two-phase on, paste-only off unless you intentionally want no new lookups.
  5. Filing PDF: Remove any chat-style title; verification and certificate of service should match the clerk’s form style — verify locally before filing.

Extra settings — skip unless you need them

Long, structured answers are automatic — you don’t need to turn anything on. The server often runs a second pass to tighten wording on substantive turns (long text, filing help, cite checks, or full citation review) — quality first; expect a bit more wait time on those replies.

Editing help: Start your message with phrases like strengthen this filing or tighten this motion to get concrete, section-by-section feedback.

Ground truth (authorities): Paste verbatim excerpts below, then press Add to vault. They attach to your next message for <quote chunk> verification — Lawbot does not invent holdings from memory. Paid databases (Lexis / Westlaw) are ideal for citator work; they are not required to use the vault. If you cannot afford them, use CourtListener, Google Scholar, and Justia to find text — paste verbatim excerpts here; nothing free replaces KeyCite/Shepard’s (see docs/OPEN_ACCESS_RESEARCH.md).

Open-access sources (verify pin cite & treatment yourself): None of these replace Shepard’s / KeyCite. Lawbot can show CourtListener forward-citation counts when COURTLISTENER_TOKEN is set — that is still not KeyCite. Serious practitioners treat free sites as finding aids, then confirm against official reporters, slip opinions, or paid research.

  • CourtListener — opinions; same ecosystem Lawbot uses for automated search
  • Official court PDFs — e.g. Supreme Court of Georgia, Court of Appeals, federal courts
  • Google Scholar (Case law) — verify reporter, court, and date
  • Justia / FindLaw — case pages; confirm the cite and holding
  • Cornell LII — federal statutes / U.S. Code
  • Georgia Code — public O.C.G.A. mirrors (e.g. law.justia.com/codes/georgia); confirm current session and official source for filing
